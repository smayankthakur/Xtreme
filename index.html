Critical issues (fix first)

📴
JavaScript-only rendering (React SPA)
Critical
  • The entire site fails to load without JavaScript — search engines and tools see blank content
  • No server-side rendering (SSR) or static generation means zero SEO indexability for a design agency
  • First contentful paint is severely delayed — users on slow mobile connections see nothing for 3–6 seconds
  • Fix: Migrate to Next.js with SSR/SSG, or add a static HTML fallback for critical pages
🙈
Weak hero section — no clear value proposition
Critical
  • Visitors can't immediately understand what Xtreme Design does and why they should stay
  • No compelling headline — the agency has 22+ years and 1200+ projects but this isn't front and centre
  • Absence of a strong above-the-fold CTA ("Get a quote", "See our work") means high bounce rates
  • Fix: Redesign hero with bold headline, a 3-second pitch, featured project image, and one primary CTA
🚫🖼️
Portfolio presentation is not doing its job
Critical
  • For a visual agency, the work samples must be large, beautiful, and impossible to miss
  • No case study format: each project needs context (brief, challenge, process, result)
  • No filtering by industry (FMCG, cosmetics, pharma, etc.) — makes browsing laborious
  • Fix: Full-bleed project tiles, hover reveals, case study detail pages per project
📣
No clear conversion funnel
Critical
  • Scattered or absent CTAs — users don't know what action to take next
  • No "Request a quote" form, no WhatsApp button, no instant contact mechanism
  • Missing lead-capture opportunity: no newsletter, no lead magnet, no consultation booking
  • Fix: Sticky header CTA, per-section CTAs, quick-contact floating widget

Phased improvement roadmap

Phase 1 — Foundation fixes (Week 1–2)
Technical and structural blockers that are hurting every visitor and every Google crawl right now.
P1Migrate to SSR / Next.js — fix the JavaScript-only rendering that blocks SEO
P1Add meta tags: unique title, description with city/niche, Open Graph, Twitter cards
P1Add schema markup: LocalBusiness, CreativeWork for portfolio items
P1Fix Core Web Vitals — lazy-load images, compress assets, use CDN
P2Set up 404 page and consistent URL structure
Phase 2 — Hero & first impression (Week 2–3)
The homepage above the fold determines whether 70% of visitors stay or leave.
P1Write a specific, outcome-driven headline. Replace generic tagline with proof of value.
P1Add sticky navigation with "Get a quote" CTA button (contrasting colour)
P2Add hero stats strip: "22+ years | 1200+ projects | 500+ brands"
P2Place 6–8 top client logos just below the fold for instant social proof
P2Feature one standout project as a full-bleed hero background or split layout
Phase 3 — Portfolio overhaul (Week 3–5)
The portfolio is the agency's primary sales tool. It must sell silently.
P1Create industry filter: All / FMCG / Cosmetics / Pharma / Beverages / Retail
P1Add hover interaction on each project tile (overlay with project name + category)
P2Build 5–8 detailed case study pages: Brief → Challenge → Process → Outcome
P2Add before/after slider for select projects to dramatise transformation
P3Add a "Similar projects" section at the bottom of each case study
Phase 4 — Trust & social proof (Week 5–6)
For B2B design services, trust is the primary conversion factor.
P2Add 5–6 testimonials with photo, name, company, and specific outcome
P2Create an awards & recognition section (any industry awards, certifications)
P2Add a "Process" page explaining how the agency works (discovery → concepts → delivery)
P3Add a Google Reviews widget or embed real ratings
P3Add an FAQ section that pre-empts buyer hesitations
Phase 5 — Conversion & contact (Week 6–7)
Lower the friction of reaching out to near-zero.
P2Add floating WhatsApp button — essential for Indian B2B audience
P2Redesign contact page: brief project intake form (3–4 fields max)
P2Add "Typical project timeline" and "What to expect" to reduce pre-sale anxiety
P3Add a Calendly or booking link for a discovery call
P3Add exit-intent popup with lead magnet (e.g. "Free packaging brief template")
Phase 6 — Content & SEO growth (Ongoing)
Turn the website into an inbound lead engine over time.
P3Launch a blog: "Packaging trends 2026", "How to brief a design agency", etc.
P3Industry landing pages: "Packaging design for FMCG brands in India"
P3Add city landing pages: Delhi, Mumbai, Bangalore — for local SEO
P3Create a free "Brand audit" or "Packaging brief" downloadable template as lead magnet
